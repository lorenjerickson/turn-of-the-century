# Campaign Generation Prep Prompt

You are designing a campaign for Turn of the Century, a gothic-horror and steampunk roleplaying game set around the 1890s. Create a campaign suitable for several linked scenarios, not a single adventure.

The campaign must feel period-appropriate: late Victorian or Edwardian in language, social concerns, technology, atmosphere, and moral unease. Blend gothic dread with industrial modernity. Prefer fog, soot, gaslight, rail stations, laboratories, hospitals, asylums, séance parlours, factories, tenements, observatories, estates, newspapers, telegraph offices, and learned societies over generic fantasy locations.

Extraordinary events must be framed through advanced science, engineering, medicine, psychology, chemistry, electricity, heredity, mesmerism, rare materials, experimental apparatus, or poorly understood natural phenomena. Do not rely on fantasy magic as an explanation. Characters may mistake strange science for sorcery, but the campaign itself should imply material causes, observable effects, limits, costs, and consequences.

## Campaign Requirements

Create a campaign with:

1. An overarching goal that gives the player characters a clear long-term purpose.
2. A central antagonist with:
   - name
   - public identity or social role
   - private concept
   - ultimate goal
   - method of pursuing that goal
   - moral or ideological justification
   - what they will do if the players never intervene
3. Starting conditions for the players:
   - where the campaign begins
   - why the characters are drawn together
   - what they know at the outset
   - what they misunderstand or cannot yet prove
   - what immediate pressure forces action
4. Session zero context for the GM and players:
   - campaign premise
   - expected tone
   - major themes
   - likely character ties
   - useful professions, backgrounds, or social connections
   - boundaries of horror, violence, class conflict, medical experimentation, and institutional corruption
   - questions the GM should ask the players before play begins
5. A structure of several scenarios:
   - each scenario should advance the campaign's central mystery or conflict
   - each scenario should have its own hook, local objective, complication, and consequence
   - scenarios should allow player agency and multiple approaches
   - failures should complicate the campaign rather than simply halt it
6. Supporting context:
   - historical or social pressures of the 1890s
   - important factions or institutions
   - recurring locations
   - recurring symbols, rumours, documents, or apparatus
   - clues that reveal the antagonist's design over time
   - escalation steps if the players delay

## Style Requirements

Write in a grave, literate, turn-of-the-century register. Use period-conscious prose without parody. Avoid modern slang, cinematic trailer language, and generic fantasy phrasing.

The campaign should evoke anxious wonder: progress casting a longer shadow than it dispels. Let dread emerge through implication, architecture, weather, machinery, institutional secrecy, family disgrace, public scandal, and scientific hubris.

## Output Priorities

The generated campaign should help a GM run a session zero and understand the campaign's long arc. It should be immediately useful as a campaign seed, with enough structure to generate linked scenarios later.

## JSON Response Requirement

Return the final result as a single valid JSON object only. Do not include Markdown fences, explanatory prose outside the JSON, or trailing commentary. The JSON must match the schema constraints supplied by the generation service.
