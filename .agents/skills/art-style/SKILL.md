# Art Style: Period-Authentic Image, Audio, and Video Assets

## Purpose

Use this skill when generating visual, sonic, or motion assets for a role-playing world shaped by gothic horror, steampunk invention, and turn-of-the-century sensibilities. The goal is to make every asset feel plausibly rooted in the late Victorian to Edwardian imagination, circa 1890–1910, rather than in modern fantasy, contemporary horror cinema, or generic retro styling.

This skill applies to:

- illustrations
- portraits
- maps
- posters and handbills
- photographs and pseudo-photographs
- ambience tracks
- musical cues
- sound effects
- voice-over direction
- cinematics
- trailers
- animated loops
- title cards
- motion backgrounds

## Core Directives

1. Keep every asset materially grounded in the period.
2. Blend gothic decay with industrial ingenuity.
3. Treat modernity as new, impressive, unstable, and morally uncertain.
4. Prefer atmosphere, texture, and implication over spectacle.
5. Avoid visual or sonic anachronisms unless they are deliberately diegetic anomalies.
6. Draw on broad period conventions, not on the recognizable signature of any one artist, composer, filmmaker, or designer.

## Overall Aesthetic

The world should feel poised between confidence and collapse. Iron, steam, gaslight, velvet, mourning crepe, polished brass, damp stone, and coal soot all belong together. Progress exists, but it is never clean. Beauty is formal, tactile, and hand-made. Horror comes not only from monsters, but from scale, age, secrecy, machinery, and inherited corruption.

Favour these aesthetic tensions:

- splendour versus rot
- piety versus blasphemy
- science versus superstition
- elegance versus grime
- industry versus mortality
- public order versus private horror

## Shared Material Vocabulary

Across all media, favour this world vocabulary:

- brass, copper, wrought iron, rivets, leather, lacquered wood, etched glass, velvet, lace, bone, ash, soot, damp stone
- gas lamps, candles, furnaces, dynamos, galvanic coils, clockwork, telegraphs, boilers, gauges, pneumatic tubes, analytical engines, airships, automata
- crypts, foundries, viaducts, railway depots, conservatories, laboratories, séance rooms, asylums, mausolea, chapels, decaying town houses
- fog, smoke, rain-slick cobbles, moonlit slate, furnace-glow, bell tones, hiss of steam, ticking escapements, distant wheels, draped interiors

## Period Guardrails

### Include

- late Victorian and Edwardian dress, silhouettes, materials, and grooming
- architecture from the period or older structures still in use
- hand-built or mechanically plausible devices
- restrained ornament, printed ephemera, engraving-like detail, and crafted surfaces
- urban fog, industrial haze, dim interiors, and practical light sources

### Avoid

- plastic surfaces, chrome minimalism, neon palettes, LED lighting, digital UI overlays, holograms, sleek sci-fi casings
- modern streetwear, tactical gear, synthetic fabrics, zippers used as a dominant style feature, modern haircuts, modern makeup styling
- action-blockbuster framing, superhero silhouettes, clean futurist interfaces, EDM sound design, cinematic bass drops, glitch aesthetics
- generic medieval fantasy, high fantasy castles, bright anime stylization, post-apocalyptic scrapheap design unless specifically requested

## Image Guidance

### Visual Tone

Images should feel haunted, formal, and tactile. The world is best conveyed through surface, light, weather, and architecture. Even portraits should imply social station, private strain, and a larger atmosphere of dread.

### Preferred Image Modes

Use one or more of these modes when suitable:

- tinted studio photograph
- cabinet-card portrait
- engraved book plate
- illustrated penny dreadful frontispiece
- scientific plate or patent illustration
- oil portrait in a dark interior
- newspaper sketch
- travel-poster or exposition-poster from the era
- lantern-slide image
- architectural study

### Composition Priorities

- strong silhouettes in fog, lamplight, doorways, or high windows
- layered depth through smoke, drapery, arches, machinery, and shadow
- asymmetry, looming architecture, narrow streets, and oppressive ceilings
- focal points driven by practical light: gas sconces, candles, furnaces, skylights, lightning, moonlight
- human figures made small against institutions, engines, towers, stations, or halls

### Colour and Lighting

Prefer palettes such as:

- sepia, umber, charcoal, lamp-black, nicotine-yellow, tarnished brass, dark green, burgundy, funeral violet, weathered ivory
- cold moonlight against warm furnace or gaslight
- muted colour with occasional concentrated highlights in brass, blood-red fabric, or green laboratory flame

Avoid:

- saturated cyber colours
- bright teal-orange blockbuster grading
- pure black shadows without texture
- glossy modern contrast that erases period materials

### Costumes and Props

Favour:

- frock coats, waistcoats, mourning attire, work aprons, gloves, watch chains, veils, cravats, spats, sensible boots, medical coats, engineer's sleeves, driving goggles used sparingly
- notebooks, surgical cases, brass instruments, field glasses, key rings, telegraph slips, lockets, canes, hat boxes, patent models, automaton limbs

Avoid costumes that read as costume-party caricature. The asset should look lived-in and socially situated, not merely decorated with gears.

### Horror in Images

Convey horror through:

- isolation
- obscured faces
- reflections and glass
- scale and distance
- evidence of past use or violence
- stillness in the wrong place
- mechanical objects that seem too animate
- domestic spaces arranged with oppressive formality

## Audio Guidance

### Sonic Tone

Audio should feel acoustic, mechanical, architectural, and atmospheric. It should suggest rooms, corridors, machinery, weather, ritual, and social restraint. The listener should sense age, enclosure, and latent menace.

### Preferred Audio Sources

Favour combinations of:

- chamber strings
- piano with period room character
- harmonium, reed organ, music box, celesta used sparingly
- low brass in restrained arrangements
- solo violin, cello, viola, clarinet, oboe, cor anglais
- choral textures that suggest chapel, mourning, or occult ceremony
- foundry rumble, steam release, ticking, telegraph clicks, train wheels, lift chains, distant bells, rain on glass, floorboard creaks, coal shovels, leather movement

### Audio Mood Design

Useful moods include:

- drawing-room unease
- industrial nocturne
- cathedral dread
- laboratory tension
- séance suspense
- railway melancholy
- aristocratic decay
- clinical horror

### Period Sound Rules

- Let instruments sound recorded in space, not hyper-compressed and modern.
- Prefer acoustic or early mechanical timbres over synthetic pads.
- Use silence and room tone as active ingredients.
- Emphasise repetition, restraint, and slow-building unease.
- If using rhythm, make it feel like machinery, footsteps, pulse, or rail motion rather than dance music.

### Avoid in Audio

- modern trailer braams
- EDM risers and drops
- glitch textures
- heavy sub-bass cinematic impacts as a default
- contemporary drum kits unless clearly diegetic and period-appropriate in arrangement
- pristine digital sheen without room noise or material texture

### Voice and Dialogue Direction

If generating spoken performance:

- aim for measured, educated diction unless class position suggests otherwise
- maintain restraint under stress
- prefer grave astonishment to shouted panic
- avoid contemporary conversational shortcuts and vocal fry
- use regional or class flavour lightly rather than exaggerated phonetic caricature

## Video Guidance

### Visual Motion Tone

Video assets should feel composed, atmospheric, and materially real. Motion should arise from weather, machinery, fabric, smoke, firelight, shadow, and human hesitation more often than from rapid action.

### Camera and Framing Priorities

Prefer:

- slow push-ins
- deliberate pans across architecture or apparatus
- locked shots with subtle environmental motion
- staged tableaux
- silhouette reveals through fog or frosted glass
- candle or gaslight flicker
- the movement of steam, drapery, train smoke, mechanical arms, pendulums, or lift cages

Avoid:

- shaky handheld modern action grammar
- quick-cut music-video editing
- drone-like impossible camera moves unless stylized as miniature or panorama work
- superhero fight blocking
- glossy CGI overstatement

### Period-Consistent Video Looks

Useful approaches include:

- lantern-slide or magic-lantern atmosphere
- silent-film-adjacent framing without requiring full silent-film parody
- early newsreel or staged photographic tableau
- dark interior cinematics lit by practicals
- aged film grain, gate weave, or projector texture used carefully

Use wear and analogue artifacts sparingly and purposefully. The asset should feel period-consistent, not distressed for its own sake.

### Editing Rhythm

- hold shots long enough for dread to accumulate
- let entrances and reveals happen at thresholds, doors, curtains, mirrors, stairheads, and lift gates
- use cutaways to details: pressure gauges, gloved hands, telegram slips, rain gutters, eyes in portrait varnish, boots on tile, a valve beginning to tremble
- end scenes on implication rather than overexposure

## Medium-Specific Prompting Guidance

### For Images

When prompting, specify:

1. subject and class context
2. location and architecture
3. period materials and props
4. lighting source
5. atmosphere and weather
6. rendering mode or historical visual treatment
7. anachronisms to exclude

### For Audio

When prompting, specify:

1. scene function or emotional purpose
2. instrumentation or sound sources
3. room or environment
4. tempo and restraint level
5. mechanical or environmental motifs
6. modern audio elements to exclude

### For Video

When prompting, specify:

1. shot type and movement
2. setting and practical lighting
3. costume and props
4. environmental motion
5. pacing and reveal strategy
6. period film treatment or realism level
7. modern cinematic habits to exclude

## Asset Design Patterns

### Portrait Pattern

1. Establish class, profession, and emotional restraint.
2. Add period clothing and one meaningful prop.
3. Place the subject in a socially revealing environment.
4. Introduce one note of dread, fatigue, obsession, or secrecy.

### Location Plate Pattern

1. Name the site and its practical function.
2. Show architectural age and current use.
3. Add weather, smoke, or light source.
4. Introduce one hidden or troubling implication.

### Ambience Pattern

1. Start with room tone or environmental base.
2. Add period machinery or architectural detail.
3. Introduce subtle musical or tonal unease.
4. Leave space for tension rather than filling every moment.

### Cinematic Loop Pattern

1. Begin in stillness.
2. Let one mechanical or atmospheric motion emerge.
3. Reveal a disquieting detail.
4. Return to unresolved tension.

## Practical Checks

Before finalizing an asset, ask:

- Does this feel physically built from period materials?
- Would the lighting, costume, and props plausibly belong to 1890–1910?
- Is the horror atmospheric rather than merely sensational?
- Is the steampunk technology mechanical and handcrafted rather than sleekly futuristic?
- Does the asset suggest class, labour, faith, science, or social order in a way consistent with the age?
- Have obvious modern visual or sonic shortcuts been removed?

## Mini Examples

### Example A: Image Prompt Flat

`A cool spooky inventor in a steampunk lab with lots of tech.`

### Example A: Preferred

`A late Victorian electrical inventor in a soot-dark laboratory, dressed in a worn waistcoat and rolled shirtsleeves, standing beside a brass-and-ebonite galvanic apparatus with pressure gauges and glass tubes; practical illumination from greenish laboratory flame and two gas sconces; damp stone walls, ledgers, tools, and telegraph slips on the bench; atmosphere of anxious scientific ambition and concealed catastrophe; rendered as a period illustration or tinted studio photograph; exclude neon, plastic, sleek sci-fi casing, and modern industrial design.`

### Example B: Audio Prompt Flat

`Dark horror ambience with creepy sounds.`

### Example B: Preferred

`Low, restrained gothic-industrial ambience for a turn-of-the-century mortuary lift descending beneath a city hospital: distant boiler throb, intermittent steam hiss, lift chains under strain, faint chapel bell, wet stone reverb, sparse harmonium and cello tones, oppressive silence between sounds, no modern trailer bass, no synthetic risers, no EDM textures.`

### Example C: Video Prompt Flat

`A dramatic cinematic shot of a haunted city.`

### Example C: Preferred

`Slow lateral pan across a fog-bound Edwardian street at midnight, gas lamps haloed in smoke, rain on cobbles, shopfront glass reflecting passing furnace-glow, a tram wire humming faintly above, one black carriage halted before a shuttered infirmary, two figures under mourning umbrellas, pacing deliberate and ominous, period-authentic costume and architecture, subtle film grain and lens softness, no modern traffic, no neon signage, no blockbuster grading.`

## Final Constraint

Every asset should feel as though it belongs to a world that reveres progress, fears what progress uncovers, and can never wholly decide whether the hiss behind the wall is steam, spirit, or conscience.
